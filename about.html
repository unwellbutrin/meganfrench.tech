About Megan French

STEM Student | Aspiring Computational Biologist | Rural Dissenter

Biography

Hello! I’m Megan French, a student of Computer Science with a divine fascination for biology, data, logical algebra, and the mysteries that connect them all. My journey began with a curiosity for how information flows, but, not just through machines, but through bodies, genes, generations, and ancient texts.

I believe the intersection of **science and spirit** is where real transformation lies. This belief has led me to a path that blends code with compassion, analytics with ancestral wisdom, and algorithms with awe.

Timeline of Goals

2025 – Started CS Associates Program

Enrolled in my first formal computer science courses at University of the People, discovering Python and the art of logical problem-solving. Set to graduate by 2027.

2027 – Start CS Bachelors Program

I will enroll at UoPeople again to earn my Bachelors Degree in Computer Science. I should have this completed by 2029.

2029 - Enroll in NYU Tandon School of Engineering - M.S in Biomedical Engineering

I would LOVE to get accepted into this program and will be working myself to the bone to do anything I can to make tuition easier on myself. I plan to apply to scholarships and save as much money as I can during undergrad to make this happen for myself.

2031 - Enroll in UC Berkeley's Bioengineering Program - PhD in Computational Biology

My dream chool. My end goal. The ultimate reason for it all. Projected time is about 5 years.

2032 - Enroll in CERN's Doctorate Student Residency Research Programme

CERN has a program for doctorate students that allows you to do a residency abroad in Geneva as part of the learning program. It would be an absolute dream come true to work alongside some of the most brilliant minds on the planet.

2037 - Work for BAE Systems, DARPA, or CERN itself

If you cant beat em, join em.

I walk with faith, but code with reason. I seek patterns in cells and in stars. My science is sacred. My research is a ritual. My goal: to bridge the biological, spiritual, and the digital for the good of humanity.
