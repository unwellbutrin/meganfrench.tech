Gladys West

One of my biggest inspirations as an aspiring computational environmental scientist and biologist.
Dr. Gladys Mae West is an American mathematician whose groundbreaking work helped make modern GPS technology possible. Born in 1930 in rural Virginia, she overcame racial and gender barriers to become one of the leading minds at the U.S. Naval Weapons Laboratory during the Cold War era.

Her most impactful contributions came through her complex mathematical modeling of the Earth's shape, known as a geoid. Her calculations were critical to the development of the satellite-based global positioning system we all rely on today — from Google Maps to autonomous vehicles.

Despite her pivotal role, her story remained largely untold for decades. Recognition came late in her life, with the U.S. Air Force honoring her in 2018 and her induction into the Air Force Space and Missile Pioneers Hall of Fame.

Dr. West’s legacy is a powerful reminder of how quiet dedication and brilliant science can shape the world. Her life inspires future generations to break barriers and reach for the stars... Or, in her case, map the planet to pinpoint accuracy.
